### Poison

Definition : Any substance given in any form (Solid/liquid/gas), any route (**Oral/injectable/inhalation/direct contact**) causing ill health or death by its local or remote action.
Rapid absorption : **Inhalational** > **Intravenous** > **Intramuscular**.

### Drug vs poison

"sola dosis facit venenum" (Given by Paracelsus) : The dose makes the poison.

## Classification of Poisons [Timestamp: 00:08:40]

mnemonic : CINCAM.


| Poison type | Features & examples |
|:---:|:---:|
| Corrosives | *   Causes corrosion. <br> *   **maximum tissue destruction.** <br> *   E.g: **Acids and alkalis.** |
| Irritants | *   Causes inflammation. <br> *   Types: <br>    *   Non metallic: Phosphorous, iodine, chlorine, bromine. <br>    *   metallic: Lead, iron, copper, arsenic, cadmium, thallium, mercury. <br>    *   Plant sources: Ricinus communis, Abrus precatorius, **calotropis**, Croton tiglium, Capsicum annuum, Semecarpus anacardium <br>    *   Animal sources: Snake bite, scorpion sting, **spider bite.** |
| Neurotoxins | *   Acting on brain (Cerebral poison): <br>    *   Somniferous (Induces sleep): Opioids. <br>    *   Deliriants (Cause delirium): Datura, cannabis, cocaine. <br>    *   Inebriants (Cause intoxication): Alcohol, chloral hydrate. <br>    *   Stimulant (Cause excitation): Caffeine, cocaine, amphetamine. <br>    *   Psychoactive: Cannabis, phencyclidine (Angel's dust) <br> *   Acting on spinal cord (Spinal poisons): <br>    *   Strychnos nux vomica: Excitation <br>    *   Gelsemium: Inhibition <br> *   Acting on peripheral nervous system: <br>    *   Conium maculatum (Hemlock) <br>    *   Curare |
| Cardiotoxins | *   Cardiac poisons mnemonic $\rightarrow$ **Car DONA**: <br>    *   Cardiac poisons <br>    *   **Digitalis (Fox glove)** <br>    *   **Oleander (Pink or yellow)** <br>    *   **Nicotine (Tobacco)** <br>    *   **Aconite (Sweet poison)** |
| Asphyxiants | *   Induce asphyxia. <br> *   Most are gases: <br>    *   Simple/inert: Carbon dioxide, nitrogen, helium. <br>    *   Chemical (Interact with cells): Carbon monoxide, **cyanide**, hydrogen sulphide. <br>    *   Irritant: Chlorine, **methyl isocyanate**, **ammonia**. |
| Miscellaneous poisons | *   Agricultural poisons: <br>    *   Insecticides <br>    *   Mosquito repellents (Pyrethroids) <br>    *   Rodenticides <br> *   Food poisoning agents |

# General Toxicology
## Diagnosis of Poisoning [Timestamp: 00:23:30]
### IN LIVING PEOPLE

1. History:
    *   Onset: Sudden/insidious.
    *   Progression: Worsens uniformly.
    *   H/o recent food/drink intake.
    *   Similar features in family members.

2. Clinical features:
    Toxidromes → Collection of signs for type of poisoning:
    *   **Cholinergic** (Organophosphates, carbamates).
    *   **Anticholinergic** (Datura, tricyclic antidepressants, anti-psychotics).
    *   **Opioids** (**morphine**).
    *   **Sedative-hypnotics**.
    *   **Sympathomimetics** (Cocaine, amphetamine, theophylline).
